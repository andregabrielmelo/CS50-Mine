Vitamins D

Strong bones

Vitamin D is a nutrient we eat and a hormone our bodies make at the same time. It is fat-soluble, and for long has be know to help the body absorb and retain calcium and phosphorus, both critical for the bones. Also, some studies show that vitamin D can reduce cancer cell growth, help control infections and reduce inflammation.

A lot of receptors for vitamin D have been found in the body, but interistingly, their roles have yet to be know. A lot of researchs have been made but the full range of vitamin D capabilities have yet to be found, but it is generally linked with disease prevention.

Few foods naturally contain vitamin D, being the best way to get enough vitamin D taking suplemments. The vitamin can be produced in the skin in the presente of the sun, more specifically, sun ultraviolet-B rays. Pigment acts like a shade, reducing the production of vitamin D in people with darker skin.
